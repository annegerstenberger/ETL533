Circulatory System

The body’s transport network of heart, blood, and blood vessels that delivers oxygen and nutrients and removes waste.

Back to top

White Blood Cells

Immune cells that find and fight germs; types include neutrophils, macrophages, and lymphocytes.

Back to top

Specialised Cells

Cells adapted for particular jobs—for example, nerve cells for signalling or muscle cells for movement.

Back to top

Nucleus

The cell’s “control centre” that stores DNA and coordinates activities like growth and division.

Back to top

Mitochondria

Organelles that release energy from food molecules—often called the “powerhouses” of the cell.

Back to top

Lysosome

A recycling and cleanup vesicle filled with enzymes that break down waste and worn-out cell parts.

Back to top

Phagocytosis

A process where a cell (like a macrophage) engulfs and digests particles such as bacteria.
