Glossary

Circulatory System

The body’s transport network of heart, blood, and blood vessels that delivers oxygen and nutrients and removes waste (Campbell, 2008).

White Blood Cells

Immune cells that find and fight germs; types include neutrophils, macrophages, and lymphocytes (Campbell, 2008).

Specialised Cells

Cells adapted for particular jobs—for example, nerve cells for signalling or muscle cells for movement (Campbell, 2008).

Nucleus

The cell’s “control centre” that stores DNA and coordinates activities like growth and division (Campbell, 2008).

Mitochondria

Organelles that release energy from food molecules—often called the “powerhouses” of the cell (Campbell, 2008).

Lysosome

A recycling and cleanup vesicle filled with enzymes that break down waste and worn-out cell parts (Campbell, 2008).

Phagocytosis

A process where a cell (like a macrophage) engulfs and digests particles such as bacteria (Campbell, 2008).
